Culture of Odisha

Festivals
Dhanu Yatra-Bargarha

Bargarh hosts the worldâ€™s largest open-air theatrical, Dhanu Yatra, annually for eleven days. It transforms into Mathura, depicting Lord Krishna's tale with Kansa. The play spans from Devaki-Vasudeva's wedding to Krishna defeating Kansa. Movable stages enhance the drama's vibrancy, making it an unforgettable spectacle.

Ratha Yatra-Puri

The Rath Yatra, originating from Puri, Odisha, is the renowned Chariot Festival. It symbolizes Lord Jagannath's annual journey to his aunt's home. Pulled by devotees, massive chariots carry Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra to the Gundicha Temple for seven days. Devotees gladly risk their lives to pull the chariots, deeming it auspicious.

Bada Yatra-Malkangiri

Manyamkonda, in Malkangiri's Kalimela Block, holds religious significance as the revered Lord Mahaprabhu is worshipped there. The Bada Yatra festival, starting from Manyamkonda every alternate year, extends to the Mauli Maa Temple. The festival honors deities Kanam Raju, Pota Raju, and Bal Raju, particularly revered by the Koya tribe.
